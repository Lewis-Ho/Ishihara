How Well Do You See Color?

Ishihara

Ishihara was a Japanese ophthalmologist who established the Ishihara Color Vision Test to detect colour blindness in 1917. Even today, his test is still commonly used to detect Daltonism (color blindness of basic colors). Daltonism, also known as color blindness. It's the inability to distinguish between certain colors. It is very common in U.S. that more than 3 million cases discovered per year. Are you sure you can perfectly differentiate between colors? How good is your color vision compare to the population?
